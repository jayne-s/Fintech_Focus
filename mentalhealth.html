Different types of Mental Health Issues

  • Anxiety : Feeling of fear, dread, and uneasiness. It might cause you to sweat, feel restless and tense, and have a rapid heartbeat. It can be a normal reaction to stress. For example, you might feel anxious when faced with a difficult problem at work, before taking a test, or before making an important decision. It can help you to cope. The anxiety may give you a boost of energy or help you focus. But for people with anxiety disorders, the fear is not temporary and can be overwhelming. Types of anxiety disorders include phobias, panic disorder, and generalized anxiety disorder.

  • Kufungisia : involves ruminating on upsetting thoughts, generating a large amount of worriying thoughts. As a cultural expression, it is considered to be causative to anxiety, depression, and somatic problems. As an idiom, it is indicative of interpersonal and social difficulties.

  • Bipolar : Associated with episodes of mood swings ranging from depressive lows to manic highs, that may occur very often. The exact cause of bipolar disorder isn’t known, but a combination of genetics, environment and altered brain structure and chemistry may play a role. Sometimes a person may feel under pressure in school, social gatherings, self awareness, all in which can lead to a person to advocately change their formal matters rapidly.

  • Seasonal Affective : A mood disorder characterised by depression that occurs at the same time every year. Seasonal affective disorder occurs in climates where there is less sunlight at certain times of the year.Symptoms include fatigue, depression, hopelessness and social withdrawal. Best treatment is light therapy with medications, that can help you dwell with your emotions over the course of the time stand.


Meditation Playlist

Meditation and mindfulness practices may have a variety of health benefits and may help people improve the quality of their lives. Recent studies have shown that meditation or mindfulness helps people manage anxiety, stress, depression, pain, or symptoms related to withdrawal from nicotine, alcohol, or opioids. Other studies have looked at the effects of meditation or mindfulness on weight control or sleep quality.
